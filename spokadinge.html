St. Petersburg United Bar Association
SPUBA in Georgia
In 2005, the Presidium of the St. Petersburg United Bar Association (SPUBA) initiated the adoption of a decision to start registering SPUBA branches in the CIS countries and the former USSR with a view to extend and introduce the Bar to the area of international relations to provide legal counsel to persons under the legal jurisdiction of the former USSR countries, though having interests in the Russian Federation. Another goal was to represent and protect the interests of Russian nationals abroad, develop foreign relations of the Bar and boost its image in the foreign market of legal support. With this in view, in the same year in September,Tbilisi witnessed the official registration of the Branch (Office) of the St. Petersburg United Bar Association in Georgia.

The main tasks of the Branch, officially approved by Georgian authorities, were representing and protecting the interests of Russian principals over a wide spectrum of issues, including those presented to state administration bodies, local administration bodies, public associations and other organisations in Georgia, and representing the interest of Georgian principals on various issues in Russia. The Branch, not being a legal entity, enjoys the rights to and bears the obligations of the Bar Association according to the Federal Law "On Legal Practice and Advocacy in the Russian Federation", the Federal Law "On Non-commercial Organisations", the Bar Association Charter, the "Provisions on the SPUBA Branch in Georgia", and generally recognised principles and norms of international law and international agreements. The Branch was set up without any limitations on its term of activity.

Georgii Chochua, lawyer and member of the Presidium and Deputy President of SPUBA for the Central Federal District of the RF, was assigned governance of the activities of foreign SPUBA branches and offices, including those in Georgia.

The scope of legal advice offered by the Branch of the St. Petersburg United Bar Association in Georgia enables building on the vast legal opportunities embodied in the legislation of the Russian Federation in Georgia to resolve economic, property and other legal disputes among the residents of both countries.
